About Y2Q

Y2Q explores how security systems survive long enough for their assumptions to fail.

Modern security thinking focuses on prevention: build better defenses, patch vulnerabilities, use stronger encryption. But prevention alone is insufficient. Systems fail. Assumptions break. Time amplifies risk.

Y2Q research examines:

  • Time as a security threat — How systems degrade, assumptions age, and dependencies rot
  • Assumption failure — What happens when core beliefs about security become invalid
  • Systemic cascading failure — How failures propagate through interconnected systems
  • Visibility and hidden dependencies — What we cannot see that threatens us
  • Survivability — How systems persist despite failure, not just how they prevent it

This is research-first thinking. Not marketing. Not sales. Honest analysis of how systems break and survive.
